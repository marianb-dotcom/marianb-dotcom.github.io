Marian BAMBICO

ARTIST STATEMENT

My work exposes collective vulnerabilities and personal insecurities in an attempt to discover authentic identity. The process of digging deeper into the recesses of our minds to investigate the roots of unwelcome thoughts and uncertainty conjures restless discomfort to the psyche. How we understand and respond to these roots lead to revelations in identity.

I began with the intent of criticizing unachievable beauty standards, focusing on the desire for pale skin in Filipino culture that was so saturated in the media during my childhood in the Philippines. Eventually, it developed into a study of my country’s colonial past, and how this has influenced the construction of present day standards of beauty. More recently, I have expanded my work to include how disparities between personal desire and cultural expectation affect one’s psyche. Ultimately, acknowledging our insecurities and understanding their source can reveal systems and perceptions in society that we want to change.
